<h1>Learning How to Learn</h1>
<h2>Overview</h2>
<p>Taught by Dr. Barbara Oakley and Dr. Terrence Sejnowski, this course provides practical insights from neuroscience about how our brains learn. With over 3 million learners, it's one of the most popular online courses ever created. It covers techniques for mastering difficult subjects, overcoming procrastination, and retaining information effectively.</p>
<h2>Learning Objectives</h2>
<p>By the end of this course, you will be able to:</p>
<ol>
<li>Understand how the brain's focused and diffuse thinking modes work</li>
<li>Apply <a href="/garden/dev_admin/concept/spaced-repetition/" class="internal-link">Spaced Repetition</a> and active recall for better memory</li>
<li>Overcome procrastination using the Pomodoro Technique</li>
<li>Recognize and combat illusions of competence</li>
<li>Use chunking to master complex subjects</li>
</ol>
<h2>Course Structure</h2>
<h3>Module 1: What is Learning?</h3>
<ul>
<li>Focused vs. diffuse modes of thinking</li>
<li>Procrastination and the Pomodoro Technique</li>
<li>Introduction to memory systems</li>
</ul>
<h3>Module 2: Chunking</h3>
<ul>
<li>How to form and master chunks</li>
<li>Illusions of competence</li>
<li>Overlearning and interleaving</li>
</ul>
<h3>Module 3: Procrastination and Memory</h3>
<ul>
<li>Tackling procrastination with habits</li>
<li>Long-term memory and <a href="/garden/dev_admin/question/how-does-sleep-affect-learning/" class="internal-link">How Does Sleep Affect Learning</a></li>
<li>Creating meaningful memory groups</li>
</ul>
<h3>Module 4: Renaissance Learning</h3>
<ul>
<li>Metaphors and analogies in learning</li>
<li>Changing your thoughts, changing your life</li>
<li>The value of teamwork and test-taking strategies</li>
</ul>

<h2>My Notes</h2>
<h3>Key Takeaways</h3>
<ul>
<li><strong>Focused mode</strong> is like a pinball machine with tight bumpers—great for familiar problems</li>
<li><strong>Diffuse mode</strong> has loose bumpers—needed for new concepts and creative solutions</li>
<li>Sleep is critical for memory consolidation (see <a href="/garden/dev_admin/question/how-does-sleep-affect-learning/" class="internal-link">How Does Sleep Affect Learning</a>)</li>
<li>Testing yourself &gt; re-reading material</li>
