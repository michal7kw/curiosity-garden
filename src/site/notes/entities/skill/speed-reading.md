<h1>Speed Reading</h1>
<h2>Overview</h2>
<p>Speed reading is a collection of techniques to increase reading speed without sacrificing comprehension. While the average person reads 200-300 words per minute (wpm), speed readers can reach 500-800+ wpm. The key is eliminating bad habits like subvocalization while building better eye movement patterns.</p>
<h2>Proficiency Levels</h2>
<h3>Novice</h3>
<ul>
<li>Reads at 200-300 wpm</li>
<li>Aware of subvocalization and regression</li>
<li>Beginning to practice pointer techniques</li>
</ul>
<h3>Competent</h3>
<ul>
<li>Reads at 400-500 wpm</li>
<li>Minimal subvocalization for easy material</li>
<li>Uses preview and chunking strategies</li>
<li>Adjusts speed based on material difficulty</li>
</ul>
<h3>Proficient</h3>
<ul>
<li>Reads at 600-800 wpm</li>
<li>Can skim effectively for key information</li>
<li>Strong metacognitive awareness of comprehension</li>
<li>Teaches others basic techniques</li>
</ul>
<h3>Expert</h3>
<ul>
<li>1000+ wpm with good comprehension</li>
<li>Pioneering new techniques</li>
<li>Published research or recognized authority</li>
</ul>

<h2>Learning Path</h2>
<h3>Phase 1: Foundations</h3>
<ul>
<li>[ ] Take baseline reading speed test</li>
<li>[ ] Practice pointer technique for 20 min/day</li>
<li>[ ] Work on reducing subvocalization</li>
</ul>
<h3>Phase 2: Practice</h3>
<ul>
<li>[ ] Read one book using speed reading techniques</li>
<li>[ ] Practice with different material types</li>
<li>[ ] Track progress weekly</li>
</ul>
<h3>Phase 3: Mastery</h3>
<ul>
<li>[ ] Integrate speed reading into daily workflow</li>
<li>[ ] Balance speed vs. comprehension for different materials</li>
<li>[ ] Teach techniques to others</li>
</ul>
